# Consent & Recording Laws

Recording a call may be legally regulated depending on where you and the
other participant are located:

- **US**: some states require **all** parties to consent ("two-party
  consent" states, e.g. California, Illinois, Washington); others only
  require one party (you) to consent.
- **EU/UK**: GDPR treats a recorded voice as personal data; informing the
  other party is the safer default.
- **India**: recording your own conversation is generally permitted, but
  sharing/using it may have separate considerations.

This tool does not verify consent for you. A simple, low-friction approach
that works almost everywhere: tell the interviewer at the start of the call,
e.g.

> "Do you mind if I record this on my end for my own notes?"

Most interviewers say yes. If they say no, stop the watcher for that call
(close the meeting app before starting it, or add a manual pause — see
`docs/run_at_startup.md` for wiring a hotkey/tray toggle).
